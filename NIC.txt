A NIC (Network Interface Card) is a hardware component inside your computer/device that makes it possible to send and receive data to/from another 
computer over a network.

    Connects your device to the network medium (cable or Wi-Fi).

    Frames data (adds MAC address, error check, etc.).
    Identifies destination using MAC address.
    Receives incoming data and delivers it to your computer.
